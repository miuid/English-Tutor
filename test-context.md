In NSW private high schools, English assessments must align with NESA English syllabuses and HSC specifications, so “what is tested” is defined by stage outcomes (Years 7–10) and Stage 6 course and exam requirements (Years 11–12).[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english)]

------

## Important caveat

NESA sets **mandatory outcomes, text requirements and exam structures**, but each private school designs its own specific tasks (e.g. which novel, which assignment type) within those rules. This means we can state with hard evidence **what kinds of knowledge and skills must be assessed in Years 8–12**, but not the exact task list for a particular private school.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/home-schooling/support-material/syllabus-overviews-and-outcome-summaries)]

## Year 8 – Stage 4 English

NESA maps Years 7–8 to **Stage 4**, with outcomes such as responding to and composing texts (EN4‑1A), using processes and strategies for texts in different media (EN4‑2A), and reading complex texts using a range of personal, creative and critical strategies (EN4‑RVL‑01). Stage 4 also requires students to analyse how meaning is created through language features and structures (EN4‑URA‑01) and examine how texts represent ideas, experiences and values (EN4‑URB‑01).[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/literacy-and-numeracy/teaching-and-learning-resources/literacy/teaching-strategies/stage-4/reading/stage-4-main-idea)]

- Reading and viewing assessments on **complex imaginative, informative and persuasive texts**, testing comprehension and analysis (linked to EN4‑RVL‑01, EN4‑URA‑01).[[schoolsequella.det.nsw.edu](https://schoolsequella.det.nsw.edu.au/file/319c3215-3c43-4557-b507-dc1694f77efc/1/continuum_compreh.pdf)]
- Extended responses where students **interpret and explain connections between their own experiences and texts, and analyse language and structure**, testing EN4‑1A and EN4‑5C.[[boardofstudies.nsw.edu](https://www.boardofstudies.nsw.edu.au/syllabus-draft-k10/pdf_doc/english-k-10-draft-syllabus-guide-v2.pdf)]
- Writing tasks where students **compose imaginative, informative and persuasive texts** using appropriate text structures, vocabulary and grammar, aligned with EN4‑1A and related writing outcomes.[[boardofstudies.nsw.edu](https://www.boardofstudies.nsw.edu.au/syllabus-draft-k10/pdf_doc/english-k-10-draft-syllabus-guide-v2.pdf)]

## Year 9 – Stage 5 (beginning)

Years 9–10 are mapped to **Stage 5**, with outcomes that extend Stage 4 to “increasingly sophisticated and sustained texts” (EN5‑1A) and “a wide range of processes, skills, strategies and knowledge” for responding to and composing texts in different media and technologies (EN5‑2A). The Stage 5 continuum also requires students to “understand and apply a wide range of reading strategies” for print, multimodal and digital texts and to “compare and contrast information within and between texts, identifying and analysing embedded perspectives.”[[theelements.schools.nsw.gov](https://theelements.schools.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/doe/sws/schools/t/theelements/localcontent/syllabus_outcomeswithinthepillarsstage5.docx)]

NESA’s Years 7–10 assessment strategies document states that teachers in Years 9–10 must provide a range of assessment opportunities directly tied to these Stage 5 outcomes, including:[[educationstandards.nsw.edu](https://www.educationstandards.nsw.edu.au/wps/portal/nesa/k-10/understanding-the-curriculum/assessment)]

- Analytical responses and essays that **critically analyse how issues, perspectives and values are represented in texts**, testing EN5‑5C and related critical thinking outcomes.[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/main-education/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/key-learning-areas/english/media/documents/english-s5-in-their-position.docx)]
- Comparative reading tasks requiring students to **evaluate supporting evidence and embedded perspectives across texts**, aligned to EN5‑2A and EN5‑5C.[[theelements.schools.nsw.gov](https://theelements.schools.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/doe/sws/schools/t/theelements/localcontent/syllabus_outcomeswithinthepillarsstage5.docx)]
- Composition tasks where students **produce sophisticated imaginative, informative and persuasive texts in print and digital forms**, meeting EN5‑1A and EN5‑2A.[[syllabus.bostes.nsw.edu](https://syllabus.bostes.nsw.edu.au/assets/englishk10/downloads/englishk10_s5.pdf)]
- Inquiry‑based research activities, presentations and performance activities, and collections of student work, identified by NESA as “additional English strategies” for Years 7–10.[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/planning-programming-and-assessing-k-12/planning-programming-and-assessing-7-12/classroom-assessment-advice-7-10-)]

## Year 10 – Stage 5 (culmination)

By Year 10 (end of Stage 5), NESA expects students to demonstrate the **full Stage 5 outcome set**, including the ability to “understand and apply a wide range of reading strategies,” “compare and contrast information within and between texts,” and “analyse and evaluate the way that inference is used in a text.” Stage 5 outcomes also emphasise posing perceptive questions, making logical predictions, and challenging ideas and information in texts, alongside sophisticated use of metalanguage to discuss aesthetic and social value.[[schoolsequella.det.nsw.edu](https://schoolsequella.det.nsw.edu.au/file/319c3215-3c43-4557-b507-dc1694f77efc/1/continuum_compreh.pdf)]

Assessment tasks in Year 10 therefore typically include, within NESA’s guidelines:[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/english/planning-programming-and-assessing-english-7-10/how-to-use-sample-english-assessments)]

- Major analytical and comparative **writing tasks and exams** on studied texts, testing sustained critical response and use of textual evidence (EN5‑1A, EN5‑5C).[[theelements.schools.nsw.gov](https://theelements.schools.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/doe/sws/schools/t/theelements/localcontent/syllabus_outcomeswithinthepillarsstage5.docx)]
- **Persuasive and multimodal tasks** on contemporary issues (for example, units on representations of refugees and asylum seekers), assessing how students compose texts that position audiences using language and structure (EN5‑7D, EN5‑9E).[[educationstandards.nsw.edu](https://educationstandards.nsw.edu.au/wps/portal/nesa/k-10/learning-areas/english-year-10/assessment-strategies/!ut/p/z1/pZLbToNAEIbfx5udPcLtllJ2gbAqYsveGKKoxNI2hfT5XfCQmFpa49ztzPfPZP5ZZNEK2U11aF6qvtluqrV7l1Y8MK0AKJDUF3MGNwHG4YxwCgsPLUfAYBUqk4OOstkc9L3gxW3oYQCOrCsLKYUiBmID0nPlItaCBRQi-NTDiZAw6s_OnxhwkZ5ILLBiOAHPxyDvGEv4AjCk4n96Tf-oN9GoN1iQ2COm4JfpJwA7be8S2RGZuuC5Hh_A9w4-Cd0ONFEpzlPCsyPgF5MngcHFn8CxTaXzwTv5D3KK8qHHbl8fgtf68Q2Vz9W6q4dc11f7Pts-1ajk7AtKm65HJVy5zK4txlhBo5vrtvVpK98BnE3Nxg!!/)]
- Speaking and performance tasks that require **oral interpretation, argument and presentation**, aligned with Stage 5 responding and composing outcomes.[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/planning-programming-and-assessing-k-12/planning-programming-and-assessing-7-12/classroom-assessment-advice-7-10-)]

## Year 11 – Stage 6 Preliminary (Standard/Advanced)

Stage 6 English (Years 11–12) shifts to **course‑based assessment requirements**, with NESA requiring schools to develop an assessment program for each Year 11 course (Standard or Advanced) based on mandatory components and weightings. For Year 11 English Advanced, NESA specifies that school‑based assessment must be split 50% for “knowledge and understanding of course content” and 50% for “skills in responding to texts and communication of ideas appropriate to audience, purpose and context across all modes.”[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-standard-stage-6-2017/assessment-and-reporting)]

NESA’s sample Year 11 assessment programs for Standard and Advanced show that students will be tested through:[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017/assessment-and-reporting)]

- At least one **formal written exam**, assessing knowledge of prescribed texts and focus areas, and the ability to write sustained analytical responses under exam conditions.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-standard-stage-6-2017/assessment-and-reporting)]
- At least one **multimodal presentation**, where students demonstrate understanding and skills across modes such as listening, speaking, viewing and representing, not only reading and writing.[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/main-education/documents/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/english/english-standard-s6-quick-reference-guide.pdf)]
- Additional internal tasks (e.g. creative writing, critical responses, integrated module tasks) designed by schools but required to assess the full range of Stage 6 syllabus outcomes and text requirements.[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/main-education/documents/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/english/english-advanced-2024-s6-quick-reference-guide.pdf)]

## Year 12 – Stage 6 HSC (Standard/Advanced)

In Year 12, students complete both **school‑based assessment** and the external HSC examination for their chosen Stage 6 English course (Standard or Advanced). NESA requires schools to submit a school‑based assessment mark for each candidate based on multiple formal tasks that reflect the same 50/50 weighting between knowledge/understanding and skills in responding and communication, including a formal written exam and a dedicated Module C “Craft of Writing” task with a 25% weighting.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/news/all/assessment-and-reporting-stage-6-english-minor-amendment)]

For English Advanced, NESA’s exam specifications define exactly what will be tested externally in the HSC:[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017)]

- **Paper 1: Common Module – Texts and Human Experiences**
  - Section I: 5–6 short‑answer questions on unseen stimulus texts, testing comprehension, analysis and interpretation of human experiences.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/hsc-standards/english-advanced)]
  - Section II: one extended response (essay) on the prescribed text for the Common Module, assessing analytical and critical writing.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/hsc-standards/english-advanced)]
- **Paper 2: Modules**
  - Section I: Module A – a sustained comparative response on prescribed texts (e.g. textual conversations).[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017)]
  - Section II: Module B – a sustained critical study of literature, focusing on a single prescribed text.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017/assessment-and-reporting)]
  - Section III: Module C – The Craft of Writing – an imaginative, discursive, persuasive, informative or reflective response that demonstrates students’ ability to craft original writing, sometimes with reflection on prescribed texts.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017)]

These exam structures, together with internal assessments, mean Year 12 students will be tested on:[[education.nsw.gov](https://education.nsw.gov.au/content/dam/main-education/documents/teaching-and-learning/curriculum/english/english-standard-2024-s6-quick-reference-guide.pdf)]

- **Close reading and analysis of unseen texts**, including understanding of language, structure and representation of human experiences (Paper 1 Section I).[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/hsc-standards/english-advanced)]
- **Extended analytical and comparative essays** on prescribed texts across modules, demonstrating sophisticated interpretation, argument and textual evidence (Paper 1 Section II; Paper 2 Sections I and II).[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/curriculum/english/english-advanced-stage-6-2017/assessment-and-reporting)]
- **Crafted original writing** in multiple forms, showing control of language, audience, purpose and style, aligned with Module C requirements and the dedicated internal assessment task.[[nsw.gov](https://www.nsw.gov.au/education-and-training/nesa/news/all/assessment-and-reporting-stage-6-english-minor-amendment)]
