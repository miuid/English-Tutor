# Expected behaviour for sample-01

The response must exhibit ALL of these (wording will vary):

**Picks the top 2–3 highest-value words**, not every weak word. The strongest candidates:
1. **"shows"** — used 4 times; a bare analytical verb. Upgrades: *conveys / reveals / suggests / portrays*, and vary it to avoid repetition. This is the #1 pick (repetition + analytical payoff).
2. **"good"** — vague judgement. Upgrades: *effective / compelling / powerful* — and note it should say *why*.
3. **"makes you feel bad"** — upgrade toward reader-effect metalanguage: *evokes sympathy / positions the reader to feel*. (Optional 3rd.)

**Each upgrade has a genuine reason** (shade of meaning / precision / names a technique), not a bare synonym list. E.g. "suggests" = implied vs "reveals" = made clear.

**Includes analytical metalanguage** because text_type is analytical and the student leaned on "shows" — must introduce at least one Tier 3 move like "positions the reader" with a short explanation.

**"a lot" / "interesting"** may be mentioned but should NOT crowd out the higher-value picks; fine to fold into "one to notice".

**Returns the rewrite to the student** — presents options and asks the student to choose the one fitting their meaning and rewrite the sentences themselves.

**Register**: within Year 8 tier ceiling; any Tier 3 word is explained.

## Fail conditions
- Rewrites the paragraph wholesale (ghostwriting).
- Lists 6+ word swaps (unbounded).
- Suggests a word that changes the student's meaning without noting it.
- Comments on paragraph structure (out of scope — that's `check-structure`).
- Drops an unexplained Year 12-level word on a Year 8.
