# Vocabulary tiers reference

Used by `elevate-vocabulary`. Based on the Tier 1/2/3 model (AERO explicit vocabulary instruction).

- **Tier 1** — everyday words (*good, said, big*). Rarely need teaching; these are the ones we *upgrade from*.
- **Tier 2** — academic words used across subjects (*convey, emphasise, significant, evident*). The main growth zone for Year 8.
- **Tier 3** — subject-specific analytical metalanguage (*connotation, juxtaposition, motif, tone*). Introduce gradually, always with meaning.

## Flat word → stronger options (with shade of meaning)

| Flat word | Analytical / academic upgrades | Note on shade |
|---|---|---|
| **shows** | conveys, reveals, suggests, illustrates, demonstrates, highlights | "suggests" = implied; "demonstrates" = proven — pick by strength of claim. |
| **says** (of a text) | states, asserts, argues, implies, contends | "implies" ≠ "states"; match how direct the text is. |
| **good / great** | effective, compelling, persuasive, powerful, skilful | name *why* it's good, not just that it is. |
| **bad** | ineffective, weak, unconvincing, flawed | analytical judgement, not opinion. |
| **big / a lot** | significant, considerable, substantial, extensive | quantify the significance. |
| **thing / stuff** | element, feature, aspect, device, technique | forces precision about what you mean. |
| **interesting** | striking, notable, provocative, compelling | "interesting" says nothing on its own. |
| **very + adj** | (drop "very", use a stronger adjective) e.g. very sad → sombre, poignant | intensity should live in the word, not "very". |
| **uses** (a technique) | employs, deploys, draws on, utilises | fine occasionally; vary to avoid repetition. |
| **makes you feel** | evokes, positions the reader to feel, elicits | "positions" introduces reader-effect metalanguage. |

## Analytical metalanguage starter set (Tier 3, Year 8)

imagery · metaphor · simile · connotation · tone · mood · symbolism · repetition · emphasis · contrast/juxtaposition · positions the reader · emphasises · foreshadows

## Rules for suggesting

1. Meaning first: never offer a word whose connotation shifts the student's point.
2. Fit the register: analytical writing is formal but not overwrought.
3. Explain any Tier 3 word the first time it's introduced.
4. Prefer replacing a repeated word or a bare analytical verb ("shows") over polishing a minor adjective.
