# QCAA A–E feedback rubric (Year 8 analytical)

Based on QCAA five-point (A–E) standard elaborations for analytical writing (reaserch.md — Evaluative Standards & Marking Rubrics). Criterion-referenced: judge against the descriptor, not against other students.

## Levels (general shape)
- **A** — purposeful, sophisticated, insightful; cohesive control throughout.
- **B** — effective, well-organised, mostly analytical; minor lapses.
- **C** — sound: correct point + evidence, but explanation thin; organisation functional.
- **D** — partial: fragments of analysis; descriptive/retelling dominates.
- **E** — minimal: little relevant analysis or structure.

## Per-criterion descriptors

| Criterion | A/B | C | D/E |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Understanding of text / ideas** | Insightful interpretation of how meaning is made | Sound, literal understanding | Superficial or inaccurate |
| **Analysis (how techniques create meaning)** | Explains effects purposefully, links technique→effect→meaning | Identifies technique + quote, explanation thin | Names or quotes with no analysis |
| **Use of evidence** | Well-chosen, embedded, analysed | Relevant but loosely integrated | Missing or irrelevant |
| **Structure & cohesion** | Clear thesis, unified paragraphs, cohesive links | Functional PEEL, some links | Disorganised, no clear point |
| **Language & vocabulary** | Precise, analytical metalanguage, controlled register | Generally clear, some flat/vague words | Imprecise, repetitive |

## Mapping to the two MVP-focus skills
- Analysis / Evidence weak → next step routes to `check-structure` (E1).
- Language & vocabulary weak → next step routes to `elevate-vocabulary`.

## The Year 8 C→A lever
Most Year 8 analytical writing sits at C because analysis is thin. The single highest-leverage next step is almost always: **explain how the technique creates its effect, before and after the quote.**
