# PEEL/TEEL structure rubric (Year 8 analytical)

Used by `check-structure` to score each element. Anchored to QCAA A–E standard elaborations for organisation of analytical writing.

## Element checks

| Element | Present & strong (A/B) | Present but weak (C) | Missing (D/E) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P — Point / Topic sentence** | Opens with a clear, arguable claim that answers the task and previews the idea. | A statement is there but is descriptive/plot-summary, or vaguely on-topic. | No controlling idea; jumps straight into a quote or retelling. |
| **E1 — Explanation / Elaboration** | Unpacks *how/why* the idea works before the evidence; shows reasoning. | Restates the point in other words without adding reasoning. | Absent; evidence is dropped in with no analysis. |
| **E2 — Evidence** | Precise, relevant quote/example, smoothly embedded, and **analysed** (linked back to the point). | Quote present but "quote-dumped" — not integrated or not analysed. | No textual evidence. |
| **L — Link** | Connects the point back to the thesis / task and signals significance. | A weak closing echo of the point. | Paragraph stops after the quote. |

## Essay-level checks (Year 8, 400–600 word extended response)

- **Thesis**: single clear position stated in the intro and sustained.
- **Paragraph unity**: one idea per paragraph, each opening with its own Point.
- **Cohesion**: linking words/logical progression between paragraphs.
- **Framing**: intro sets up the argument; conclusion synthesises rather than repeats.

## Priority order for choosing the ONE focus gap

1. Missing or unclear **Point** (nothing else can be judged without it).
2. **Evidence without Explanation** (E2 present, E1 missing) — the #1 Year 8 analytical weakness: quote-dumping.
3. Missing **Link**.
4. **Wrong order** of otherwise-present elements.

## The Year 8 → A+ move

The gap between a C and an A at Year 8 analytical writing is almost always the **E1 (explanation/analysis)**: C-grade students state a point and prove it with a quote but never explain *how the language creates the effect*. Coaching toward "explain the effect of the technique before and after the quote" is the highest-leverage structural intervention.
