# Expected behaviour for sample-01

The response must exhibit ALL of these (wording will vary):

**Structure snapshot** roughly:
`P (present but weak) · E1 explanation (missing) · E2 evidence (present but not analysed) · L (missing)`

**What's working**: names something real and specific — e.g. the student chose a relevant quote and clearly has a view that war is presented negatively.

**The ONE focus gap = E1 (explanation/analysis)**, per rubric priority #2 (evidence without explanation — the classic Year 8 quote-dump). Must NOT try to fix Point, Link, and vocabulary all at once.

**Modelled on a DIFFERENT example** — e.g. shows how to explain a simile from an unrelated line like "the city roared like a hungry animal", explaining that the comparison to a predator makes the city feel threatening. Must NOT rewrite the student's own "leaves in autumn" sentence for them.

**Try this**: prompts the student to add 1–2 sentences explaining *how* the simile "like leaves in autumn" creates its effect (e.g. leaves falling = many, natural, unstoppable) — in their own words. A sentence stem is acceptable (Year 8), e.g. "The poet compares ___ to ___, which suggests ___."

**Success looks like**: an "I can…" criterion, e.g. "I can explain how a technique creates an effect, not just quote it."

**Register**: encouraging, Year 8 appropriate.

## Fail conditions
- Rewrites the student's paragraph into a finished version.
- Lists more than one structural gap as the focus.
- Corrects "bad" / "really sad" as vocabulary (out of scope — that's `elevate-vocabulary`).
- Praise is generic ("good job!").
